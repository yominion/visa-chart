Visa Sponsorship Pathway Chart

Immigration & Mobility Reference · For Managers, Org Leadership & People Operations
✓ Cap-Exempt Organization Research / Education Nonprofit Last Updated: March 2026 Nonimmigrant + Immigrant Pathways
🏛️
Your organization qualifies for H-1B cap exemption under INA §214(g)(5).

This means no lottery, no April 1 filing window, and no October 1 start-date requirement for H-1B petitions. You may file and begin employment year-round — a significant advantage over for-profit employers. This exemption applies to nonprofit research institutions and nonprofits affiliated with or related to institutions of higher education.

Note: As of September 19, 2025, per Presidential Proclamation (Proclamation 10973), a $100K supplemental fee is now applicable for certain new H-1B petitions, requiring employers filing for workers outside the US (consular processing) after 9/21/2025 to pay the fee as a condition tied to the petition/entry process. The requirement does not apply to existing H-1B holders, renewals, or petitions filed before that date. Further guidance is available from USCIS and the White House proclamation text.

H-1B

Specialty Occupation — Cap-Exempt

Most common work visa. Your org bypasses the annual lottery entirely.
Technical Non-Technical Cap-Exempt ✓
▼
🎯 Eligibility Criteria
  • Role requires a theoretical and practical application of highly specialized knowledge
  • Position normally requires at least a U.S. bachelor's degree (or equivalent) in a specific specialty field
  • Candidate must hold that degree (or its equivalent) — foreign degrees acceptable with evaluation
  • Employer must qualify as cap-exempt (nonprofit research institution or affiliated with university) — confirmed ✓
  • Prevailing wage must be paid per the Department of Labor wage determination for the role/location
  • Technical: Engineers, researchers, data scientists, computational roles — strong fit

4

5
If abroad: consular processing (DS-160 + interview at U.S. consulate); if in U.S.: change/extend status
6
Post public access file at principal place of business

O-1A

Extraordinary Ability — Sciences, Education, Business

Top-tier nonimmigrant visa for distinguished researchers, scientists, and senior technical talent.
Technical ✓ (Primary) Non-Technical (Senior only)
▼
🎯 Eligibility Criteria
  • Must demonstrate extraordinary ability in sciences, education, business, or athletics — sustained national or international acclaim
  • Must meet at least 3 of 8 regulatory criteria (or comparable evidence): awards; membership in prestigious associations; published material; critical role at distinguished org; high salary; original contributions; scholarly articles; high remuneration
  • No degree requirement (but a strong record of achievement required)
  • No nationality restriction; no annual cap; no lottery
  • Best fit: Principal Investigators, senior researchers, distinguished engineers, faculty-equivalent roles, postdoc-to-PI transitions
  • Non-technical applicability: Possible for exceptional executives or widely published business experts; bar is high; consult counsel

Gather extraordinary ability evidence: awards, publications, citation counts, recommendation letters from experts
2
Obtain advisory opinion from a peer group / expert (technically required but often waived by USCIS)
3
Prepare and file I-129 with O-1 supplement and evidence package
4
If abroad: consular processing; if in U.S.: change/extend status

Legal Fees (est.)

$5,000–$12,000

Timeline
2–5 months

🚩 Flags & Notes
✅No cap, no lottery, no nationality restriction. Strong path for top researchers when H-1B eligibility is unclear.
📋Initial period: up to 3 years. Extensions in 1-year increments. No statutory maximum duration.
📋O-1 is employer-specific (tied to petitioner). A new petition is needed for each employer.
⚠️Evidence assembly is time-intensive. Plan 4–8 weeks to gather materials before filing. Build candidate profiles early.
🔬Requires further review for non-technical roles. Business operations managers rarely meet the threshold without a strong publication/award record.

🏛️ Org-Level & Category-Level Exemptions / Special Provisions ▼

🏛️ H-1B Cap Exemption — INA §214(g)(5)

Your organization is cap-exempt if it is:

  • An institution of higher education under HEA §101(a)
  • A nonprofit entity related to or affiliated with such an institution
  • A nonprofit research or governmental research organization

Effect: No annual H-1B lottery. File year-round. No April 1 filing deadline. No October 1 start date requirement. Concurrent employment possible across multiple cap-exempt employers.

✅ No PERM for EB-1A / EB-1B / EB-2 NIW

For research organizations, the most qualified researchers often qualify for EB-1B or NIW, bypassing the PERM labor certification entirely. This eliminates:

  • 12–18 months of PERM processing
  • $5,000–$10,000 in additional legal costs
  • The risk of PERM audit and denial
  • Recruitment compliance complexity

Recommendation: Default to EB-1B or NIW for all research staff green card filings where possible.

🔄 H-1B Portability (AC21 — INA §204(j))

Once an I-485 has been pending for 180+ days and the I-140 has been approved, the employee may:

  • Change to a same or similar position
  • Change employers (including leaving your org)
  • Remain eligible for green card under original priority date

This provides employees with significant job flexibility. Communicating this to hiring managers helps frame green card sponsorship as an investment, not a lock-in.

⚠️ J-1 Two-Year Home Residency Waiver

The INA §212(e) two-year home residency requirement may be waived through:

  • No Objection Statement: Home country government waives objection
  • IGA (Interested Government Agency): A U.S. federal agency (e.g., NIH, NSF) supports waiver for research of national interest
  • Hardship Waiver: Exceptional hardship to U.S. citizen/LPR spouse or child
  • Conrad 30 State Program: Physicians in underserved areas (healthcare only)

IGA waivers are most applicable and effective for research org employees with federally-funded research. Engage immigration counsel early.

📋 Concurrent H-1B Employment

An employee already on an H-1B with another employer may concurrently hold an H-1B with your organization:

  • File a new cap-exempt H-1B petition (no cap issue for your org)
  • Employee may begin work upon filing if currently in valid H-1B status (portability)
  • Does not require terminating prior employer's H-1B

Useful for recruiting active employees who hold H-1B status elsewhere, or part-time/adjunct arrangements.

🎓 F-1 Cap-Gap Extension

When an F-1 OPT/STEM OPT holder has an H-1B petition filed and approved, a "cap-gap" extends OPT status through September 30. Since your org is cap-exempt, the standard cap-gap rules still technically apply, but your cap-exempt H-1B can start immediately — no waiting for October 1. This allows continuous employment without interruption.

🌍 Priority Date Preservation (EB-1B / NIW)

I-140 approval establishes a priority date even if I-485 is not yet filed. Key implications:

  • File I-140 early for all employees on long-term employment track
  • Approved I-140 survives employer change (AC21) if I-485 is pending 180+ days
  • Priority date is not lost if employment is terminated after I-140 approval (absent fraud/misrepresentation)

🚨 Prevailing Wage — Still Required

Cap-exempt status does NOT waive prevailing wage requirements for H-1B. Your organization must still:

  • Obtain a certified LCA (Labor Condition Application) per role/location
  • Pay the higher of the actual wage or the prevailing wage for the area of employment
  • Maintain a Public Access File (PAF) at principal place of business
  • Notify co-workers of H-1B filing via posting or notice

Wage violations can result in fines, debarment from future H-1B filings, and back pay obligations.
